# Task 5: Monthly Charges & Tenure Heatmap Analysis

## Objective
To analyze the relationship between Monthly Charges, Tenure, and Customer Churn using a Pivot Table and Heatmap in Microsoft Excel.

## Description
This task visualizes the interaction between Monthly Charges, Tenure, and Customer Churn by creating a heatmap using Conditional Formatting. The heatmap highlights patterns in average monthly charges across different tenure levels, making it easier to identify customer behavior and churn trends.

## Dataset Information
- **Dataset Name:** Telco Customer Churn Dataset
- **Total Records:** 7,043 Customer Records
- **Attributes Analyzed:** MonthlyCharges, Tenure, and Churn

## Tasks Performed
- Created a Pivot Table using the Telco Customer Churn dataset.
- Calculated the average Monthly Charges for each tenure level.
- Categorized customers based on Churn (Yes/No).
- Applied Conditional Formatting to generate a Heatmap.
- Formatted the table with professional headings and color scales.
- Analyzed the relationship between tenure, monthly charges, and churn behavior.

## Pivot Table Configuration
- **Rows:** Tenure
- **Columns:** Churn
- **Values:** Average of MonthlyCharges

## Heatmap Overview
The heatmap uses color intensity to represent the average monthly charges across different tenure levels and churn categories. Higher values are highlighted with darker shades, making trends easy to identify.

## Key Insights
- Customers who churned (Yes) generally have higher average monthly charges than customers who did not churn.
- Higher monthly charges are consistently observed across most tenure levels for churned customers.
- The average monthly charges of non-churned customers remain comparatively lower and more stable.
- The heatmap clearly highlights variations in monthly charges across different tenure groups.
- Customers with higher monthly charges appear more likely to churn.
- The visualization helps identify pricing patterns that may influence customer retention.

This analysis demonstrates how heatmaps can effectively visualize relationships between multiple variables. The findings indicate that customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to churn, providing valuable insights for improving customer retention strategies.
